# Affiliate Disclosure

**Last updated:** June 2026

Some links on tamirdresher.com are affiliate links. This means that if you click on a link and make a purchase, I may receive a small commission at no additional cost to you.

## What This Means for You

- **You pay the same price** whether you use my affiliate link or not. The commission comes from the seller, not from you.
- **I only recommend products and services I personally use and trust.** If I link to a tool, it's because I use it in my daily work as a Principal Engineer at Microsoft. If I haven't used it, it's not on this site.
- **My opinions are my own.** Affiliate relationships do not influence my reviews or recommendations. If a tool isn't good, I won't recommend it regardless of commission. Life's too short for bad tools.

## Programs I Participate In

- **Amazon Associates** — Links to books and hardware on Amazon.com
- **Manning Publications Affiliate Program** — Links to Manning books (including my own — yes, I get affiliate credit for my own book, and no, that doesn't make me feel weird about it)
- **Commission Junction (Pluralsight)** — Links to Pluralsight online courses and learning paths
- **JetBrains Content Creators** — Links to JetBrains products (Rider, ReSharper, and other tools)

## Why Affiliate Links?

I spend significant time creating free content — blog posts, tool reviews, open-source contributions, and an upcoming book. Affiliate revenue helps offset the time and hosting costs involved in maintaining this site, without putting any content behind a paywall.

The goal is simple: keep the content free, keep the recommendations honest, and maybe cover the cost of my Azure bill. (Spoiler: the Azure bill always wins.)

## How to Identify Affiliate Links

Affiliate links on this site are typically marked with a disclosure note at the bottom of each post. In most cases, they link to products on Amazon, Manning, Pluralsight, or JetBrains.

## Questions?

If you have questions about any product recommendation on this site, feel free to reach out. I'm happy to explain why I use any tool I recommend — and equally happy to tell you when a free alternative is just as good.
