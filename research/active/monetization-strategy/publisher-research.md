# Book Publisher Research & Recommendations
## "Squad: Building an AI Team That Works While You Sleep"

**Author:** Tamir Dresher (Principal Engineer, Microsoft)  
**Book:** 8 chapters, ~280 pages, completed with images/screenshots  
**Focus:** AI agent teams using GitHub Copilot CLI + Squad framework (.NET/Azure/Microsoft ecosystem)  
**Previous Publications:** Manning ("Rx.NET in Action"), Packt ("Mastering Microsoft Endpoint Manager")

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This research evaluates 8 publishers plus self-publishing for Tamir's AI agents book. Given his Microsoft background, established author platform, and deep Microsoft-ecosystem focus, there are **3 standout opportunities:**

1. **🏆 #1 RECOMMENDATION: O'Reilly Media** — Top-tier tech publisher with strong AI/agents catalog, proven royalties, and fastest path to market
2. **🏆 #2 RECOMMENDATION: Microsoft Press / 8080 Books** — Perfect strategic fit but requires internal connections; potentially highest prestige
3. **🏆 #3 RECOMMENDATION: Pragmatic Programmers** — Exceptional royalties (42%) but no upfront advance; best for backend income

**KEY INSIGHT:** Tamir can (and should) submit to multiple publishers simultaneously with disclosure. Having both a completed manuscript AND strong platform (Microsoft, Azure expertise) significantly improves negotiating power.

### 3. **PRAGMATIC PROGRAMMERS** — ⭐⭐⭐⭐ HIGH ROYALTIES

**Submission Process:**
- Email proposal to `proposals@pragprog.com`
- Use their proposal template (available on website)
- Include: scope, audience, chapters, author bio
- Very author-friendly; responsive team

**Advances & Royalties:**
- **Advance:** ❌ **NONE** (no upfront payment)
- **Royalties:** 42% for first-time authors (negotiable upward)
  - **This is 2.5–4x higher than traditional publishers**
- **Print & eBook royalties:** Both at 42% (no differentiation)
- **Payment:** Royalties earned from first sale onward (no recoupment wait)

**Timeline to Publication:**
- Proposal to contract: 2–4 weeks (fast decision)
- Manuscript development: 3–6 months
- Total: 5–10 months
- **Significantly faster than O'Reilly**

**Existing AI/Agent Books:**
- Limited AI/agent titles; more focused on developer productivity, testing, Ruby
- Strong in practical, hands-on developer books

**Why It's Good (Not Perfect) for This Book:**
- ✅ Exceptional royalty rates reward successful sales
- ✅ Minimal upfront costs; no advance recoupment
- ✅ Fast publication timeline
- ✅ Supportive editorial process
- ❌ Smaller publisher = less marketing support vs. O'Reilly
- ❌ No upfront advance (risky if book sells poorly)
- ❌ Less ideal for immediate monetization (backend-heavy model)

**Contact:**
- Email: `proposals@pragprog.com`
- Website: https://pragprog.com/publish-with-us/

**Fit Assessment:** ✅ **GOOD** (Best for long-term royalty income, not upfront cash)

### 8. **SELF-PUBLISHING: AMAZON KDP + LEANPUB** — ⭐⭐⭐ CONTROL & SPEED

**Amazon KDP (Kindle Direct Publishing)**

**Royalties & Margins:**
- **eBook:** 35–70% (70% if priced $2.99–$9.99 in select territories)
- **Print:** Up to 60% royalty minus printing costs
- **Audiobook (ACX):** 10–70% depending on exclusivity

**Timeline:**
- Publication: 72 hours (immediate)
- Royalty payments: Monthly (60 days after sale month)

**Leanpub (Direct Sales)**

**Royalties & Margins:**
- **Direct Leanpub sales:** 80% of revenue (highest in industry)
- **Via Amazon (premium service):** 80% of net revenue after Amazon's cut ($2,499 fee)

**Timeline:**
- Instant for direct publishing
- ~14 days for Amazon concierge service

**Advantages:**
- ✅ **Highest margins:** 70–80% vs. 10–15% traditional
- ✅ **Full control:** Pricing, cover, updates, marketing timing
- ✅ **Fastest to market:** Days, not months
- ✅ **Continuous updates:** Ideal for fast-evolving AI/tech topics
- ✅ **Direct audience relationship:** Newsletter, direct sales

**Disadvantages:**
- ❌ **No advance:** You earn only from sales
- ❌ **No marketing support:** You handle all promotion
- ❌ **Highly competitive:** Amazon/Leanpub marketplaces are crowded
- ❌ **No editorial support:** Self-editing/proofing responsibility
- ❌ **Prestige:** Self-published books lack traditional publisher credibility

**Why It's Attractive for Tamir:**
- He has a platform: Microsoft reputation, blog, newsletter, research institute
- Fast-moving topic: AI agents tech changes rapidly; self-publishing allows quick updates
- Hybrid option: Publish on Leanpub first (build audience), then license to traditional publisher

**Fit Assessment:** ✅ **GOOD** (Best as secondary/complementary strategy, not primary)
