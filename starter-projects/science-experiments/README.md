## 🚀 Getting Started (For Parents)

### Step 1: Print the Journal Template
Open `elsa-science-lab.md` and scroll to the **Science Journal Template** section at the bottom. Print one copy per week (10 total), or let your child create their own journal with blank paper.

### Step 2: Read Ahead Each Week
Before each experiment, read through the full experiment together. Check that you have all the materials.

### Step 3: Safety Notes
- **Hot water** is used in Weeks 1, 2, and 8. Always help with boiling or very hot water.
- **Cutting** is required in Week 3 (celery) and Week 10 (plastic bottle). Use scissors or a knife yourself.
- **Alka-Seltzer** in Week 4 is optional — plain fizzing bath tablets work too.
- All other steps are **child-safe and child-led**.

### Step 4: Encourage the Journal
After each experiment, give your child 5–10 minutes to complete their journal page — even just drawing is great! The journal builds scientific thinking habits.

### Step 5: Celebrate!
After Week 10, print or copy the **Certificate of Completion** at the end of the curriculum and fill it in together. 🎉

These experiments are designed to support common 3rd grade science topics including:

- **NGSS 3-LS4** — Biological Evolution: Unity and Diversity (Weeks 3, 6, 7, 10)
- **NGSS 2-ESS2** — Earth's Systems: Processes That Shape the Earth (Weeks 4, 8)
- **NGSS 5-PS1** — Simple chemical reactions (Weeks 6, 7) *(introduced early — excellent enrichment!)*

## 💡 Tips for Success

- **Do one experiment per week** — this gives time for journaling and discussion
- **Let your child lead** — you're the safety helper, they're the scientist!
- **Ask "why do you think...?" questions** — don't give the answer right away
- **Take photos** — a photo journal alongside the written one is wonderful
- **Connect to books** — pair with animal books from the library each week
