## What This Area Does

The marketing area owns **content strategy, editorial assets, and distribution planning** for
the Squad framework and related projects. It is the creative and communications hub for the team.

Key responsibilities:
- **Content strategy** — long-term editorial calendar, topic prioritization, audience segmentation
- **Blog posts & articles** — drafts for dev.to, LinkedIn, Hashnode, and other platforms
- **Social media content** — Twitter/X threads, LinkedIn professional posts, hook headlines
- **Distribution coordination** — `distribution-calendar.md` tracks cross-platform publish schedule
- **Cross-posting guidance** — adapted content per platform (dev.to cross-posts, LinkedIn formatting)

The marketing area is primarily **content files** (Markdown). It does not contain code. Changes
here affect external-facing brand and developer relations, so tone, accuracy, and timing matter.

## Routing Hints

- **New content drafts** → Troi authors; Picard reviews for accuracy on technical claims
- **Distribution scheduling** → Neelix coordinates timing and channel selection
- **LinkedIn / professional tone** → Troi primary; human review recommended before publishing
- **Technical accuracy in posts** → Picard or Data fact-check before publish
- **Brand/voice decisions** → Troi owns; major pivots require Picard sign-off
- **Cross-posting adaptations** → Troi adapts; Neelix schedules delivery

## Constraints

- Do not publish claims about performance, security, or compliance without Data/Picard sign-off
- Do not include internal project codenames or unreleased feature names in external posts
- Keep `distribution-calendar.md` updated when posts are published — it is the single source of truth for scheduling
- Coordinate with Neelix before any time-sensitive distribution (announcements, launches)

## Conventions

- All content files are Markdown (`.md`)
- Drafts are committed as-is; publishing is a separate step tracked in `distribution-calendar.md`
- Hook headlines go in `hook-headlines.md` for reuse across platforms — don't bury them in per-post files
- Platform-specific adaptations live in separate files per platform (e.g., `devto-crosspost-part1.md`)
