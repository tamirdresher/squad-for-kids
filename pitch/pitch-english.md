## The Problem (You Already Know This One)

You have kids. You've seen it firsthand:
- Schools move at one pace. Your kid might need a different one.
- Tutoring costs $50-100/hour. That's $200-400/month per subject.
- Educational apps are either boring (worksheets on a screen) or entertaining but shallow (games that teach nothing).
- You, as a parent, want to help — but you can't be an expert in every subject, and frankly, your kids don't want you to be their teacher anyway.

**150 million kids** in the US and Israel alone are in K-12 education. Most of them are underserved by a system designed for the average, not the individual.

## The Solution: Squad for Kids

An AI squad — a team of 4-6 specialized agents — that becomes your child's personal learning team.

Not a chatbot. Not a tutor app. A **team of characters** that your kid actually wants to talk to:
- **Coach** makes math feel like a game
- **Story** turns reading into an adventure
- **Explorer** does science experiments together
- **Buddy** helps with friendships and feelings

Each agent has a distinct personality, teaching style, and expertise. They adapt to the child's age (6-18), interests (dinosaurs? Minecraft? K-pop?), and pace. They never get frustrated, never judge, and never run out of patience.

## Why This Works

1. **Kids already talk to AI.** They're using ChatGPT, Character.AI, and others — with zero safety guardrails. We give them something better.
2. **Parents want this.** Every parent we've talked to says the same thing: "I wish someone could help my kid with homework patiently." This is that someone.
3. **Teachers want this.** Teachers are overwhelmed. A squad that handles practice, review, and enrichment frees them to focus on what they do best.
4. **The tech is ready.** LLMs are good enough. Copilot's squad architecture is perfect for this. We're not building new AI — we're packaging existing capability for a new audience.
