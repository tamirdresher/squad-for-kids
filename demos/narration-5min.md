# 🎙️ Narration Script — 5-Minute Quick Demo

> **Style:** Casual, enthusiastic, like demoing to a friend. Not a sales pitch.
> **Audience:** Developers, educators, tech-curious parents
> **Delivery:** Voice-over or live narration while screen-recording

---

## [0:00] Opening

> "Alright, let me show you something I've been really excited about.
>
> This is Squad for Kids — it's an AI learning system that gives every
> child their own personalized team of tutors. But here's the twist:
> the tutors are cast as characters from whatever the kid loves most.
>
> Let me show you what that looks like in practice."

---

## [0:15] Starting Up

> "So I'm a parent. I open my terminal in the Squad for Kids repo,
> and I just say 'hi.' That's it.
>
> The system sees there's no student profile yet, so it kicks off
> the onboarding flow. Watch how simple this is."

---

## [0:30] Onboarding — Name & Birthday

> "It asks for the kid's first name — 'Yoav.'
> Then birthday — 'March 12, 2018.' It calculates the age automatically.
>
> Notice it only asks for first name and birthday. No last names,
> no addresses, no phone numbers. Privacy by design."

---

## [0:50] Onboarding — Grade & City

> "Grade — '2nd grade.' City — 'Rishon LeZion.'
>
> Now watch what happens. From just the city name, it auto-detects
> that we're in Israel, looks up the Israeli Ministry of Education
> curriculum for grade 2, and pulls in all the subjects:
> Math, Hebrew, Reading, Science, English, Bible Studies, and more.
>
> The parent didn't have to configure anything. One city name, and
> the system figured out the entire educational framework."

---

## [1:30] Language Choice

> "Because it detected Israel, it suggests Hebrew. But we say
> 'English please' — and from here on, everything's in English.
>
> It supports ten-plus languages. Arabic, Spanish, Japanese —
> whatever you need. And you can switch at any time."

---

## [2:00] Universe Pick

> "Now here's where it gets really fun. It asks: 'What's your
> favorite movie, show, book, or game?'
>
> Yoav says 'Harry Potter.' And watch what happens next..."

---

## [2:15] Team Reveal

> "BOOM. The entire learning team gets cast as Harry Potter characters.
>
> Hermione Granger is the Head Teacher — because who else would
> organize lesson plans and track curriculum progress?
>
> Hagrid is the Science Helper — the guy who loves magical creatures
> and knows everything about nature.
>
> Luna Lovegood is the Creative Coach — because creativity IS seeing
> things differently.
>
> Fred Weasley is the Gamer — because he'd absolutely turn homework
> into a game.
>
> George Weasley is the YouTuber — he'd make the most entertaining
> science videos.
>
> And Dobby... Dobby is the Study Buddy. Always there. Always
> believes in you. Unconditional support.
>
> This works with ANY universe. Say 'Minecraft' and you get Alex
> the Redstone Engineer teaching math. Say 'Frozen' and Elsa's your
> teacher. The characters match the roles naturally."

---

## [3:30] First Math Lesson

> "Now Yoav says 'let's do some math,' and Hermione takes over.
>
> Look at the problem: 'If you have 7 chocolate frogs and Hagrid
> gives you 8 more...' It's grade 2 addition within 20 — straight
> from the Israeli curriculum. But it's wrapped in Harry Potter
> language that makes Yoav WANT to solve it.
>
> The kid thinks they're doing Hogwarts math. The parent knows
> it's aligned to national standards. Everybody wins."

---

## [4:30] Parent Report

> "And finally — the parent view. At any point, you can ask for
> a report. It shows sessions, time, subjects, XP, strengths,
> areas for practice, and specific recommendations.
>
> 'Practice multiplication during car rides.' 'Read Harry Potter
> together.' Actionable, personalized, useful.
>
> This report also syncs to a parent squad automatically —
> we'll see that in the full demo."

---

## [4:50] Closing

> "That's Squad for Kids in five minutes. A full onboarding,
> a personalized team, a curriculum-aligned lesson, and parent
> visibility — all from a GitHub repo with a single agent file.
>
> The full 10-minute demo covers the Gamer playing Quidditch Math,
> the YouTuber explaining rain, what happens when a kid says
> 'this is too hard,' automatic grade transitions, and Hebrew mode.
>
> But the core idea is this: every kid deserves a team that speaks
> their language — literally and figuratively. Squad for Kids
> makes that happen."
